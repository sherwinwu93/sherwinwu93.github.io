I'm always indeliberately saying something which makes people around me unhappy.

I'm sorry to hear that you're feeling upset about unintentionally causing discomfort to those around you.
It's not uncommon for communication challenges to arise, and it's a part of human interaction.
Here are some suggestions to help you navigate these situations:

Reflect on the Situation:
Take some time to think about the specific instances where you believe you may have upset someone. Try to understand the context, your words, and how they might have been interpreted.

Practice Active Listening:
Pay close attention to what others are saying and respond thoughtfully.
Sometimes, misunderstandings can be avoided by actively listening and ensuring you understand others' perspectives before expressing your own.

Choose Your Words Carefully:
Be mindful of your language and how it might be perceived.
Avoid making assumptions or generalizations that could be hurtful.
Think before you speak, especially in sensitive or emotional situations.

Empathy:
Put yourself in others' shoes and try to understand their feelings.
Showing empathy can go a long way in building positive relationships and preventing misunderstandings.

Ask for Feedback:
If you feel comfortable, you can ask for feedback from people you trust.
Let them know that you are working on improving your communication and would appreciate their input.

Apologize When Necessary:
If you realize that you've said something unintentionally hurtful, don't hesitate to apologize.
A sincere apology can go a long way in repairing relationships.

Learn and Grow:
Treat each interaction as an opportunity to learn and grow.
Identify patterns in your communication that may be causing discomfort, and work on improving those aspects.

Consider Professional Help:
If you find that your communication challenges are significantly impacting your relationships and well-being, it might be helpful to seek guidance from a therapist or counselor.
They can provide personalized strategies to improve your communication skills.

Remember that everyone makes mistakes, and the important thing is to learn from them and strive to improve.
It's also okay to seek support from friends, family, or professionals as you work on enhancing your communication skills.
